Global CO2 Emissions Analysis

CO2 Emissions by Fuel Type

During the year 2021, emissions from burning coal contributed the most to CO2 emissions at the time. Coal contributed to 40.4% of the overall growth in global CO2 Emissions while Flaring contributed the least, amounting to only 1.1%. Not to mention, it is now at an all-time high of 15.3 Gt, surpassing the previous peak observed in 2014 by almost 200 Mt.

CO2 Emissions Over Time

From the first ever recorded date of the year 1750, where CO2 emissions started to become apparent, this was when the Industrial Revolution started. Since then, CO2 emissions have increased up to almost 37 Gigatonnes, which is approximately 37 billion metric tonnes. Since 1990, global CO2 emissions have increased by more than 60 percent.

Countries CO2 Emissions

China has the highest CO2 emission, amounting to almost 12 billion metric tonnes. With a population of 1,412,360,000, their total CO2 emissions per capita is roughly 8.05 tonnes. However, if we were to look at Qatar, they had about 95.6 million tonnes. On the other hand, per capita they were releasing 35.6 metric tons per person.
